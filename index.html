Money and financial support

Calculate your severance pay

If your job has ended, your employer may owe you money. This tool tells you how much.

What you need to know first

Have these ready before you start:

  • Why your job ended.
  • How many complete years you worked there.
  • Your usual pay each week, or each month.

How much time does it take?

Takes about 3 minutes.

Start now

This tool can help if

  • You worked for the same employer for at least 2 years.
  • You were 16 to 64 years old on your last day at work.
  • Your job ended because it was cut, a natural disaster, or you had no work for a long time.

This tool may not help if

  • You were fired because of your behaviour.
  • You said no to a similar job from the same employer.
  • Your job type is not covered by the law.

When to ask for your money

You must ask within 12 months of your last day at work. If you wait longer, you may lose your right to claim.

After you see your estimate

This is the minimum your employer must pay under the law. Your contract may give you more.

If they do not pay, contact the Ministry of Labour and Social Partnership Relations.
